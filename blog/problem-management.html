Why problem management fails in growing organisations

Governance • Operational Risk • Board Oversight

As organisations scale, incidents become more frequent — but paradoxically, the ability to learn from them often declines.

Problem management fails not because teams lack intelligence or intent, but because ownership becomes blurred, escalation weakens, and governance mechanisms lag behind growth.

In many boardrooms, recurring issues are discussed repeatedly without ever being formally classified as problems requiring root-cause resolution.

The result is a cycle of temporary fixes, firefighting, and increasing operational risk — until a regulatory event, audit failure, or customer impact forces attention.

Strong problem management requires discipline: clear accountability, documented causes, corrective actions, and preventive controls that boards can see and track.

Without this, organisations don’t just experience failures — they institutionalise them.
